Speed to Lead

Speed to lead is a tracked KPI. The clock starts the second the new-lead notification lands, and it only stops when someone calls. Unless you are on a live call, a fresh lead outranks whatever you are doing.

Claim It, Call It, Log It

Tracker
  • Claim it. Notification comes in and nobody has taken ownership? It is yours. Say so where the team can see it so two people do not call the same lead.
  • Call it now. Minutes, not hours. A lead called back immediately picks up; the same lead an hour later is a voicemail.
  • Log it immediately. Open the lead in the tracker (use + New lead if it is not there yet) and log the touch. That first logged attempt is your speed-to-lead record. A call that is not logged did not happen, and an unlogged claim means the lead sits unowned.

No answer? The cadence from station 3 takes over: it goes to +1 day automatically and stays in your queue.

Log Every Touch, Let the Cadence Work

Every call, text, email, or DM gets logged on the lead card the moment it happens. The tracker schedules your next follow-up for you based on the outcome. Never keep follow-ups in your head.

The Follow-Up Cadence

Automatic
Outcome you logNext follow-up

You can override the date on the card when a lead asks for a specific day. Otherwise, trust the cadence.
